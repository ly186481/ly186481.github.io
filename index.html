EXH. 01Profile

About

I hold an M.A. in Economics and work at the intersection of applied econometrics, digital finance, and business strategy. My research background is in labor economics — using duration and survival analysis to study how unemployment length relates to mental health outcomes — and I bring the same evidence-driven approach to commercial strategy work: frame the stakeholder's question precisely, choose the right analytical method, and translate results into a decision.

On the strategy side, I've worked on national-level policy positioning (data governance in the G20 context), e-commerce and retail expansion analysis, and customer-segmentation dashboards built for non-technical stakeholders.

How I work

  1. Frame the ask. Pin down the actual decision a stakeholder needs to make.
  2. Choose the method. Match the analytical tool to the question — survival models, association rules, segmentation.
  3. Analyze the data. Work from raw, often messy data to a defensible result.
  4. Deliver the decision. Translate findings into a brief, dashboard, or recommendation a non-technical stakeholder can act on.

EXH. 03Thesis

Research

M.A. THESIS · ECONOMICS

Mental Health and Unemployment Duration: Evidence Using Duration Analysis

This thesis applies duration (survival) analysis to examine how the length of unemployment relates to mental health outcomes, modeling the hazard of exiting unemployment over time rather than relying on a single cross-sectional snapshot. The approach follows the logic used across labor economics and biostatistics: track a cohort over time, estimate the probability of an event occurring in each period, and test how covariates shift that risk.
